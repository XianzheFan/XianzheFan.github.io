Xianzhe Fan 范娴喆 @ Tsinghua

Hi!😊 I am a senior undergraduate in the Tsien Excellence in Engineering Program (TEEP) at Tsinghua University. I am honored to be advised by Professor Maarten Sap at the Language Technologies Institute, Professor Hong Shen and Professor Haiyi Zhu at the Human-Computer Interaction Institute at Carnegie Mellon University, and Professor Zhicong Lu at George Mason University. Previously, I was fortunate to be advised by Professor Chun Yu and Professor Weinan Shi at Pervasive Interaction Lab, Department of Computer Science and Technology, Tsinghua University.

My research interests lie in the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and Artificial Intelligence (AI). I develop systems based on large language models (LLMs) and multimodal models, aiming to reshape the ways users collaborate and communicate with AI, while exploring AI's potential impacts in areas such as artistic creation, social interaction, algorithmic justice, office automation, and journalism. I also build context-aware mobile computing platforms and design intelligent, human-centered sensing systems.

Research topics:

  • Human-AI Interaction & Ubiquitous Computing: Creativity Support Systems, Context-Aware Systems, LLM-Based Multi-Agent Systems, Retrieval-Augmented Generation Systems, Speech Recognition and Noise Reduction, Robotic Process Automation
  • Social Computing & Responsible AI: Value Alignment & Social Bias, Trustworthy & Explainable AI
  • Multimodal Models: Large Vision-and-Language Models, Diffusion Models
